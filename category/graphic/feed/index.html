Change

An habit forming app with a focus on sustainability.

Details

Role: UX Designer, UX Researcher

Client: Computer Human Interaction Competition 

Time frame: October 2019 – January 2020 

Techniques

  • Sketching
  • Data Collection
  • Affinity Diagram
  • Prototyping

Tools

  • Figma
  • Adobe Illustrator 
  • Miro

Details

Role: UX Designer, UX Researcher

Client: Computer Human Interaction Competition Competition 

Time frame: October 2019 – January 2020 

Techniques

  • Sketching
  • Prototyping
  • Affinity Diagram 
  • Data Collection

Tools

  • Figma
  • Adobe Illustrator 
  • Miro

Summary

I worked in a team to develop a sustainable habit-forming application for the 2020 Computer Human Interaction (CHI) Competition.


To start the process, we focused on data collection. I conducted several interviews along with a literature review. This information was combined with my teammate’s data via an affinity wall in Miro.


We then analyzed the affinity wall and started to sketch ideas for what the app could be/include. I sketched 10 ideas. My ideas were combined with my teammates to develop a final concept.


With the identified concept in mind, each team member focused on developing prototypes for specific sections of the application. Each member developed a low- and a mid-fidelity prototype, In Figma. We combined our prototypes to develop a high-fidelity prototype.


After a few refinements to the high-fidelity prototype, we finalized and submitted our proposed application to the CHI competition.

Process

Empathize

Problem Statement

In a perfect world, a person’s habits would be sustainable. Unfortunately, people can contribute to climate change with their unsustainable habits. Because of this, millions and billions of tons of food waste, CO2e, and plastic waste are generated every year.

Situation

The Computer Human Interaction (CHI) competition required participants to gamify a traditionally non-gaming field. Fields such as education, mental health, and sustainability. My group chose to focus on sustainability. The following are statistics that support our choice:
  • “In 2017 alone, almost 41 million tons of food waste were generated“
  • “In 2017, passenger cars and light-duty trucks were responsible for 770 million metric tons CO2e and 327 million metric tons CO2e“
  • “by 2050, there will be 12 billion metric tons of plastic in landfills“

Define

Goals & Objectives

To change users’ habits to reduce the amount of waste they produce and help them build sustainable habits.

Discovery

My group and I conducted, recorded, and noted several interviews of potential users. We also sought professional counsel from an expert in the habit formation field.

Findings

From the interviews and professional advice, my team organized our findings via an affinity diagram in Miro.

Ideate

Solutions

From the affinity wall, my group found that users would be more likely to practice sustainable habits if it was convenient, supportive, and if others around them did sustainable actions.

Brainstorming & Sketching

We each had several rounds of sketching to propose ideas and concepts; here are some of my sketches. We reviewed and voted on what ideas we thought were best.

Prototype

Each member conducted several rounds of prototyping that were later combined into one final high-fidelity prototype 

Low Fidelity

I created mid fidelity prototype in figma.

Mid Fidelity

I created mid fidelity prototype in figma.

Future

To prepare for the upcoming CHI competition my group combined our prototypes and other required materials. Other material I contributed was a trailer (a team member created a new logo, high-fidelity prototype, and recording of said prototype shown in the video below) Other possibilities include further refinement and testing of the application, seek funding and create a start-up.

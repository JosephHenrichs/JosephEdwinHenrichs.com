A kiosk design to reduce a city’s panhandling population

Summary

For my introduction to interactive design, I created a kiosk designed to get panhandlers off the street.
The panhandlers would be replaced by kiosks, which would collect donations from patrons. The donations will then be gathered by local homeless shelters and related non-profits and distribute gift cards equally between each participating panhandler.


My first step was finding an issue to solve. I thought it would be best to tackle an issue that affects my local community. Ann Arbor, Michigan has a large panhandling problem. I thought it was time to address it.Next step was a competitive analysis of the competition. Locally, I discovered that the competition ranged from traditional local non-profits to a unique public relations campaign.


What followed was sketching potential solutions, storyboarding ideal situations where my product could be used, and personas of my target market. Then I conducted several interviews with users of my target market. Finally, I crafted a paper, mid-fidelity digital and a final high-fidelity prototype.

Process

Empathize

Problem Statement

Downtown Ann Arbor is a bustling and profitable neighborhood. Yet, some are not thriving financially as panhandling is a pressing issue for patrons and residents of the area. Thus, causing a rise in crime and prejudice against a vulnerable population.

Situation

In 2010, Ann Arbor Police Chief Barnett Jones told the Ann Arbor City Council that aggressive panhandling had become the No. 1 crime in Ann Arbor.

Define

Objective & Goals

  • Aggressive panhandling has become a problem in Downtown Ann Arbor,
  • Business owners and consumers view panhandlers as a nuisance.
  • Law enforcement must deal with complaints about panhandlers.
  • Many would like to assist panhandlers with obtaining basic needs while preserving the dignity of the panhandler.

Discovery

This situation requires social and financial intervention. The City of Ann Arbor, and several non-profits, attempted various solutions to the panhandling problem. They have tried law enforcement, a public relations campaign, and homeless shelters

Findings

Most people report a willingness to help panhandlers from time to time. Business owners, customers, and law enforcement look on panhandlers as a nuisance.

Ideate

Solutions

The current/attempted/proposed solutions are…
  • “Have a Heart, Give Smart” public relation campaign
  • Non-profit homeless shelters
  • Stricter revisions of Ann Arbor’s solicitation ordinance
  • Universal Basic Income (UBI)

Brainstorming

Some of my solutions that I brainstormed are…
  • A kiosk that replaces panhandlers physically
  • An adapted check holder at local restaurants requesting donations for panhandlers
  • Parking meter-like equipment for collecting donations
  • “Cash – for – Cans” Recycling bin where the “Cash” would be distributed among panhandlers
  • Vending machine that sells panhandler’s products

Results

A donation kiosk is viable solution to the panhandling problem in Downtown Ann Arbor. A donation kiosk allows willing supporters to contribute funds to the needy for distribution later through nonprofit organizations.   The kiosk offers those who want to assist panhandlers an opportunity to contribute in any amount through a preferred method.  Panhandlers receive needed support while maintaining their personal dignity. With panhandlers removed from the streets, local businesses should see a boost in customer activity.

User Testing

Originally, the proposed design resembled a vending machine from which the panhandler could receive donations directly for needed support. After three user tests, the concept changed to a medium between non-profits, panhandlers, and patrons.

Considerations & Refinement

Several user testing sessions indicated that those who donated to panhandlers wanted to ensure that their contributions were used wisely for necessities like food and shelter, not alcohol or street drugs. Thus, the vending machine became a donation kiosk

Future

The course final was to submit the final prototype and present my product. In addition, I also created a logo in Adobe Illustrator for The Helping Hand. 

While business owners, customers, and law enforcement workers may appreciate the kiosk for controlling panhandling activities, there are considerations that could affect implementation by the city.  For example, where should kiosks be located? Who will attend them? How will nonprofit organizations obtain funds from the kiosks and disperse funds? Details will need to be studied for future implementation.

Summary

For my Micromasters Capstone course, I developed an app proposal which tracks receipt acquisition by either taking a photo of the receipt or linked to a debit/credit/mobile payment. 

I began with data collection. I conducted several interviews of potential users. With this data, I created a persona, scenario, and an affinity wall.

I the proceeded to sketch concepts of what the app should be. These later became wireframes, where I conducted several user tests.

After the testing, I refined it with several levels of prototypes, more testing, and a produced a heuristic evaluation.

In the future, I could pursue an entrepreneurship route by creating a start-up around and pitch the idea to investors . 

Process

Empathize

Problem Statement

Consumers require receipts. Receipts are important for tax purposes and for employee expense reports. Receipts may also provide evidence of fraudulent charges on consumer credit cards

Situation

  • Managing receipts can be frustrating.
  • Managing receipts can be time consuming.
  • Paper receipts affect the consumer’s environment, given the number of trees and the amount litter generated by paper receipts.

Define

Objective & Goals

People have a difficult time keeping track of their receipts. Receipts are often considered nuisance scraps of paper that are stuffed into wallets and purses only to disappear when needed.

Discovery

  • Paper receipts are easily lost and difficult to duplicate after ink fades on them, coffee spills on them, or some other mishap befalls them.
  • Consumers usually track and compile spending manually from receipts, which can be inaccurate and time consuming.
  • The environmental impact of paper receipts negatively affects the consumer’s physical environment. Approximately 9.6 million trees are used annually to manufacture the 640,000 tons of paper made into receipts (Warnick, 2012).

Ideate

Solutions

An application is needed that will eliminate or limit the need for paper receipts and provide a systematic approach to managing information on receipts easily and effectively.

Findings & Brainstorming

Affinity Wall

Sketching

Prototype

Test potential users using a paper prototypes to perform specific tasks; for example, scanning receipts, labeling and editing data, and exporting data to software. Participants considered the accuracy, ease, and speed of each of these tasks. The researcher repeated the process with Mid-fi and Hi-fi prototypes.

Wireframe

Paper Prototype

Test

Results

The researcher systematically established the quality, performance, or reliability of prototypes

User Testing

  • The researcher recorded data from the participants’ task trials on a logging form.
  • The researcher also provided a step-by-step process for preparing for and conducting the user tests.
  • The researcher reviewed the user test process with participants upon completion of the test.

Considerations & Refinement

Changes were made to screen designs and navigation based on feedback during user testing.

Future

Users favored the application in testing. The application appears to have potential as a product for consumers. Further refinement and testing continue.
